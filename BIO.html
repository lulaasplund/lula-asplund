B. 1998, LULA ASPLUND IS AN EXPERIMENTAL COMPOSER AND PERFORMER OF ELECTROACOUSTIC MUSIC WORKING BETWEEN CHICAGO AND THE BAY AREA.

ASPLUND’S WORK BRINGS TOGETHER VISCERAL SONIC ELEMENTS OF PSYCHOACOUSTICS AND SPECTRAL SYNTHESIS TO EXPLORE DREAM WORLDS, MEMORY PALACES, ALCHEMY, AND WONDER. WHISPERS ARE DISGUISED AND LATTICED TOGETHER INTO AN INTIMATE WEB, HOPING TO EXTEND A GLIMPSE INTO A VIGNETTE BARELY HELD TOGETHER BY STRINGS.

SOMEWHERE AMONG AMBIENT, PSYCHEDELIC, SPECTRAL, AND EXPERIMENTAL, ASPLUND WORKS WITH SOUND AS RAW MATERIAL. SHE WORKS WITH BOTH DIGITAL AND ANALOG TECHNOLOGY BETWEEN "LO-FI" TAPE AND DIGITAL "GLITCH"; ASPLUND'S EXPERIMENTS EXPLORE CYBERNETIC IDEAS AND COMPLEX FEEDBACK SYSTEMS TO CREATE GENERATIVE MUSIC, AND DIGITAL INSTRUMENTS IN MAX AND PURE DATA.

ASPLUND HOLDS A BFA IN ELECTRONIC MUSIC FROM MILLS COLLEGE, WHERE SHE STUDIED WITH MAGGIE PAYNE, JOHN BISCHOFF, ZEENA PARKINS, AND LAETITIA SONAMI. SHE LATER RECEIVED HER MFA IN SOUND STUDIES FROM THE SCHOOL OF THE ART INSTITUTE OF CHICAGO, STUDYING WITH NICOLAS COLLINS, BOB SNYDER, AND SHAWN DECKER.

ASPLUND ALSO CREATES IMMERSIVE INSTALLATIONS FOR MULTI-CHANNEL SOUND SYSTEMS AND SITE-SPECIFIC PERFORMANCE SPACES, HAVING PERFORMED IN VENUES SUCH AS ELASTIC ARTS (16-CHANNEL SOUND), BOND CHAPEL, BOHEMIAN NATIONAL CEMETERY, THE LAB SF, SAIC GALLERIES, DRAG CITY HEADQUARTERS, AMONG OTHERS. ASPLUND’S MUSIC HAS BEEN FEATURED BY NPR MUSIC, THE QUIETUS, NEW NOISE MAGAZINE, STEREOGUM, AS WELL AS FOXY DIGITALIS.
